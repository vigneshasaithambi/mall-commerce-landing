# Mall Commerce Landing Page — Requirements (v3)

**URL:** fynd.com/solutions/mall-commerce
**Audience:** Mall operators and retail conglomerates
**Page goal:** Demo bookings from mall operators and multi-brand retail groups
**Core narrative:** Omnichannel mall commerce — online fills the mall with footfall, the mall fills the cart with online revenue. One platform, one loop.

---

## Part 1 — Target Audience

### 1.1 Primary: Mall Operators & Retail Real Estate
Decision-makers at Nexus Select Trust, Phoenix Mills, Prestige, DLF Retail, Lulu Group, Landmark Group (MEA), MAP/Mitra Adiperkasa (SEA). Real estate operators earning via lease rentals, CAM, and revenue share.

**They care about:** footfall (physical + digital), unified digital identity, giving tenants a digital channel, centralised cross-brand data, mall-wide loyalty (the moat), AI-driven optimisation.

**Vocabulary:** tenants (not brands), footfall (not traffic), GLA, trading density, occupancy, anchor tenants.

### 1.2 Secondary: Retail Conglomerates
Reliance Retail, ABFRL, Landmark, Tata. Own multiple brands, want a unified digital destination with central control + brand autonomy.

### 1.3 ICP

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Company type | Mall operator, retail REIT, or conglomerate |
| Scale | 3+ malls, OR 1 mall with 50+ tenants, OR 5+ consumer brands |
| Geography | India (primary), MEA + SEA (secondary) |
| Revenue model | Lease rental + CAM + revenue share |
| Mall grade | Grade A with national/international tenants |
| Digital maturity | Already runs loyalty app or mall website |
| Decision maker | COO, VP Operations, VP Digital/Marketing, Head of Retail |
| Not the buyer | CTO/IT |

**Buying triggers:** tenants asking for digital, D2C/marketplace pressure, existing loyalty app proving demand, recent capex, multi-city portfolio.

**Disqualifiers:** standalone retail, <10 tenants, pure online marketplace, no digital intent, can't commit to enterprise pricing.

---

## Part 2 — Why a Dedicated Page

- No platform positions as "mall commerce"
- Mall operators already know this is broken (Nexus 247 deck: broken journeys, wait fatigue, offline-online divide, fragmented systems, missing personalisation)
- Existing mall apps (NexusONE, Phoenix) are loyalty-first, not commerce-first
- No competitor pitches AI-native mall commerce

### Fold 2 — Run the whole mall from one screen (Operator)
1. **One dashboard for the entire mall** — Orders, traffic, conversion, and basket data across every tenant. Mall level and brand level views. All in real time.
2. **AI that tells you what to do** — AI surfaces insights in plain English. What changed, why it matters, and what to do next.
3. **One profile per shopper** — Unified customer view across every tenant. Basket affinity, visit frequency, and brand crossover in one place.
4. **Run offers and promotions across the mall** — Trending lists, seasonal campaigns, and personalised feeds for every tenant. Managed from one place, not store by store.
5. **Onboard tenants in hours, not days** — Bring new tenants online fast. AI builds their storefront. Shared checkout, loyalty, and logistics are ready from day one.
6. **Connects to your existing systems** — Works with the ERP, WMS, and POS systems your tenants already use. Real time stock visibility. No rip and replace.

### Fold 3 — Online fills the mall (Online → Offline)
**Subheading:** Every online action is designed to bring a customer through the door.
1. **Buy online, pick up at the mall** — Customers order from home and collect from the mall. Every pickup is a footfall. Every footfall passes other stores.
2. **Buy online, return at any store** — Returns happen in the physical mall, not through a courier. Every return is a visit. Every visit is a chance to buy again.
3. **Digital coupons redeemable in mall** — Online purchases generate coupons that can only be redeemed at a physical store. Converts online shoppers into foot traffic.
4. **Loyalty points for movies and events** — Points earned from shopping can be redeemed for movies, spa, gaming, and events in the mall. Turns shoppers into visitors.
5. **Book movies, events, and experiences** — Movies, spa appointments, gaming zones, and events bookable through the digital mall. Every booking is a confirmed visit.
6. **Reserve parking before you arrive** — Book a parking slot and pay online. The biggest friction point of visiting a mall, removed before the customer leaves home.

### Fold 4 — The mall fills the cart (Offline → Online)
**Subheading:** Every visit to the physical mall turns into online revenue that lasts long after they leave.
1. **Same-day delivery from the mall** — Customer visits once, then shops the same stores from home. Products delivered from the mall to their door the same day.
2. **Scan in store, buy online** — Product not on the shelf? Scan it, browse the full catalogue via endless aisle, and buy online for home delivery.
3. **AI search and discovery from anywhere** — Search across every tenant at once. AI personalises results. Shop the look, watch product videos, see what is trending in the mall.
4. **One cart across every tenant** — Saw something in three stores? Add them all to one cart from home. One checkout, one invoice, no friction.
5. **Food court pre-ordering** — Order food online before arriving or while shopping. Skip the queue. F&B tenants get digital orders without building their own app.
6. **Rewards that keep them coming back** — One loyalty programme across every tenant and every channel. Points earned in the mall work online. Points earned online work in the mall.

### Fold 5 — Every tenant goes digital on day one (Tenant)
1. **A storefront without hiring a developer** — Any tenant goes live on your domain. AI builds storefront pages from a plain language description.
2. **Customers they would never reach alone** — Cross-brand discovery, AI recommendations, and endless aisle let customers find products across the full catalogue.
3. **Same-day delivery they could never afford** — The mall provides shared delivery infrastructure. A local boutique gets the same delivery speed as a national chain.
4. **Products visible to AI assistants** — Every tenant's catalogue automatically becomes readable by AI assistants. Free traffic, no ad spend required.
5. **Works for fashion, electronics, and food** — Not just retail. F&B tenants get online ordering and pre-order to skip the queue.
6. **Level playing field** — Shared checkout, shared loyalty, shared logistics. The smallest tenant gets the same infrastructure as the biggest.

### Fold 6 — How it works
1. **Set up your mall** — Choose your domain, set design guidelines, invite tenants.
2. **Brand stores go live** — Configure layouts, use AI to build storefront pages.
3. **Customers shop online and show up** — Buy online and pick up at the mall. Redeem coupons in store. Book movies and reserve parking. Online drives footfall. Footfall drives online.
4. **AI takes over** — The system learns from every visit. Recommendations get smarter, promotions perform better, revenue compounds.

### Fold 7 — Who is this for
- **Shopping mall operators** — You manage a physical mall with 50+ tenants. You want to connect the online and offline experience so every digital action drives footfall and every visit drives online revenue. *Listed REITs, regional mall groups, developers with retail assets.*
- **Retail conglomerates** — You own multiple brands and want them on one platform. Each brand keeps its own identity. You get one dashboard, shared logistics, and customer data that works across the portfolio. *Multi-brand groups across fashion, beauty, electronics, home, or grocery.*
