## Part 1 — Target Audience

### 1.1 Primary: Mall Operators & Retail Real Estate
Decision-makers at Nexus Select Trust, Phoenix Mills, Prestige, DLF Retail, Lulu Group, Landmark Group (MEA), MAP/Mitra Adiperkasa (SEA). Real estate operators earning via lease rentals, CAM, and revenue share.

**They care about:** footfall (physical + digital), unified digital identity, giving tenants a digital channel, centralised cross-brand data, mall-wide loyalty (the moat), AI-driven optimisation.

**Vocabulary:** tenants (not brands), footfall (not traffic), GLA, trading density, occupancy, anchor tenants.

### 1.2 Secondary: Retail Conglomerates
Reliance Retail, ABFRL, Landmark, Tata. Own multiple brands, want a unified digital destination with central control + brand autonomy.

### 1.3 ICP

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Company type | Mall operator, retail REIT, or conglomerate |
| Scale | 3+ malls, OR 1 mall with 50+ tenants, OR 5+ consumer brands |
| Geography | India (primary), MEA + SEA (secondary) |
| Revenue model | Lease rental + CAM + revenue share |
| Mall grade | Grade A with national/international tenants |
| Digital maturity | Already runs loyalty app or mall website |
| Decision maker | COO, VP Operations, VP Digital/Marketing, Head of Retail |
| Not the buyer | CTO/IT |

**Buying triggers:** tenants asking for digital, D2C/marketplace pressure, existing loyalty app proving demand, recent capex, multi-city portfolio.

**Disqualifiers:** standalone retail, <10 tenants, pure online marketplace, no digital intent, can't commit to enterprise pricing.

---

## Part 2 — Why a Dedicated Page

- No platform positions as "mall commerce" — Shopify Plus, BigCommerce, Adobe, Salesforce, commercetools all frame multi-storefront for brand owners, not mall operators.
- Existing mall apps (NexusONE, Phoenix) are loyalty-first, not commerce-first.
- No competitor pitches AI-native mall commerce.

**Mall Commerce vs Curated Marketplace** — different buyer (COO vs founder/CTO), different vocabulary (tenants/footfall vs sellers/GMV), different starting point (existing tenants vs sellers to recruit), different physical presence requirements. Must be separate pages.

### Fold 2 — Run the whole mall from one screen (Operator)
1. **One dashboard for the entire mall** — Orders, traffic, conversion, and basket data across every tenant. Mall level and brand level views. All in real time.
2. **AI that tells you what to do** — AI surfaces insights in plain English. What changed, why it matters, and what to do next. No more chasing reports from individual tenants.
3. **Unified logistics and returns** — One pickup hub for all tenants. Buy online, return in any physical store. Connects to existing ERP, WMS, and POS.
4. **A mall that keeps getting better** — AI runs experiments and deploys winners automatically. Revenue compounds across tenants without adding headcount.

### Fold 3 — One mall. One seamless shopping experience. (Customer)
1. **One mall, every store inside** — Customers browse across every tenant on one domain and shop the way they walk through your building.
2. **One cart, one checkout** — Buy from multiple tenants in a single transaction. One cart, one invoice, zero friction at brand boundaries.
3. **Products they actually want** — AI recommends products from other tenants automatically. A shoe buyer sees socks. A skincare buyer sees fragrances.
4. **Rewards that work everywhere** — Earn points at any tenant, redeem at any other. One wallet, one loyalty programme for the entire mall.

### Fold 4 — Every tenant goes digital on day one (Tenant)
1. **A digital storefront without hiring a developer** — Any tenant goes live on your domain. AI builds storefront pages from a plain language description. No designer, no developer, no waiting.
2. **Customers they would never reach on their own** — Cross-brand discovery and AI recommendations route customers into stores they would not have found by themselves.
3. **Products visible to AI assistants** — Every tenant's catalogue automatically becomes readable by AI assistants. Free traffic, no ad spend required.
4. **Promotions powered by the mall** — Mall-wide campaigns and personalised feeds drive footfall to every tenant, not just the anchor brands.

### Fold 5 — How it works
1. **Set up your mall** — Choose your domain, set design guidelines for consistency, and invite tenants to join.
2. **Brand stores go live** — You decide how each brand store looks. Set design guidelines, configure layouts, and use AI to build storefront pages from a plain language description.
3. **Customers shop the mall** — Browse across tenants, add to one cart, check out once, earn mall-wide points. All on your domain.
4. **AI takes over** — The system learns from every visit. Recommendations get smarter, promotions perform better, and revenue compounds across tenants.
